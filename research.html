Research

Understanding mobility through behavior, surveys, and networks.

My work develops empirical and modeling approaches for transportation research, from the quality of reported trips to mobility patterns across people, places, and modes.

Travel survey quality

I study possible trip misreporting in household travel surveys and its relationship with response diligence. My doctoral work develops indicators based on item nonresponse, open-ended questions, proxy responses, incentive seeking, and submission timing, then integrates these signals with behavioral models in a bias-correction framework.

Methods and data: household travel surveys; response-diligence indicators; behavioral models.

Related work: Travel Behaviour and Society (2026); Transportation Research Part A (2026); JST SPRING doctoral project.

Older adults, license surrenders, and mobility

I examine heterogeneity in older adults’ travel purposes and frequencies, including the roles of self-rated health and car-use status. Current work separates license surrender intention from actual behavior and investigates socio-demographic and built-environment determinants, as well as mobility impacts.

Methods and data: latent class analysis; travel behavior data; socio-demographic and built-environment measures.

Related work: CICTP conference proceedings (2024); JST SPRING research project.

Time-varying multimodal networks

I model metropolitan mobility as a time-varying multimodal network with temporal links between layers. An extended PageRank approach evaluates regional importance while addressing the underestimation of peripheral areas in conventional representations.

Methods and data: complex network analysis; multimodal mobility networks; PageRank; temporal coupling.

Related work: Physica A (2025); master’s thesis at Kumamoto University.

Built environment and urban function

I am interested in how built environments shape travel demand and license surrender behavior, and how spatial systems constrain urban functional layouts. This theme connects transportation policy analysis with accessibility, spatial analysis, and the spatio-temporal dynamics of urban functional zones.

Methods and data: QGIS and ArcGIS; statistical modeling; spatial and built-environment measures.

Related work: license surrender project; manuscript in submission on ecological zones and functional layout.
